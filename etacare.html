ETACare App

Task Management App for Caregivers and Clients

ETACare Task Management App

EtaCare is a task management app that aims to allow caregivers, physicians, and patients to be able to communicate, track medication, create to-do lists, and set appointments to simplify and organize their daily routines.

Problem

People in need of assistance have trouble keeping track of their schedule, remembering their doctor appointments, forgetting when to take their medication, and knowing who to communicate with when they feel helpless.

  • For one, there is a lack of apps that don’t allow cross-communication from clients to caregivers to physicians.
  • This is important to have access to important medical information, care plans, and organization.

Solution

The app addresses individuals in need of assistance who often struggle to manage their schedules, recall doctor appointments, medication schedules, and determine whom to contact during moments of vulnerability.

  • Existing solutions fall short, lacking integrated communication channels connecting clients, caregivers, and physicians.
  • The app fills this gap by facilitating cross-communication, ensuring accessibility to crucial medical information, care plans, and overall organizational support.

My Design Process

Research and Users

Research Process

During the research process, I wanted to find users who found issues with the apps they were using or wanted an app that could benefit them in their everyday lives. So I conducted a survey that asked questions to record users' mobile app habits, what they use, and what they like and dislike about it. I learned that users liked apps to be fast, organized, and feasible. This part of the research was difficult due to having to stay on topic, but I had the user just show me how they use their favorite app and least favorite app and then describe out loud their thought process. I then had them record their answers after finishing the tasks.

Ideate Process

In the ideate process, I went back to look at the answers the users recorded and made a list of mobile app ideas I found interesting, but also where there was a need for improvement. I wrote down a category of people who I found needed to be represented and mobile apps that could be developed. I then looked over the responses to my survey and collaborated on ways to combine the user responses of what needs improvement or what they would like to see (task management, goalkeeping, note-taking). Next, I researched existing mobile apps that mirrored the direction I was going toward and overlooked case studies from product designers who created similar apps.

Usability Testing

For the usability testing, I gathered up my previous users and had them test the final prototype of the mobile app. I first observed their first impression of the app, then we did a usability test based on tasks and scenarios. The goal of these scenarios were to see how a user will go through the process of creating a tasks, updating their schedule, and sending notifications to shared profiles. This represents the main functions of the mobile app.

Tasks & Scenarios

Please Log In to the app, upon seeing your dashboard, you need to create a new task, can you please show me how you will add a new task to your to do-list?

  1. Expected Path: Click on “add” icon.
  2. Expected Path: Click on Create after seeing the entered information.
  3. Expected Path: Confirmation Screen

You want to update your current doctor’s appointment and change the time to 10:30 to 11:00 am, how would you update your schedule?

  1. Expected Path: Click 'See more’ to choose the date (optional since it’s showing already in to-do list).
  2. Expected Path: Click on the Appointment in the Today lists.
  3. Expected Path: Make sure time is changed and click ‘Update’

Now you want to alert your family and friends on the changes you made, how would you do that?

  1. Expected Path: Click Send Notifications to shared profiles
  2. Expected Path: Choose Shared Users
  3. Expected Path: Click Send
  4. Expected Path: Get Confirmation of notifications

Results & Solutions

I found that there were a few issues the users had ran into when doing the usability testing

  1. Highlighted tasks that aren’t being clicked or visually doesn’t make sense to the users.
  2. The app may be too bright for some users. There were complaints about how bright the screens were.
  3. There are too many ways to complete the same task. There are unnecessary screens for creating a to-do list or adding to the calendar.

Solution #1

Instead of highlighting every other task, it should highlight the tasks that are upcoming or being clicked. This visually helps to keep track of what tasks needs to be done next. Also helps with clicking and editing. With also combing this with a darkmode feature.

Solution #2

There are too many ways to complete the same task with unnecessary screens for creating a to-do list or adding to the calendar. The add to tasks button takes you to the calendar dashboard, that should be removed and take you to the create a tasks screen. Additionally, there’s a “Schedule” portion that needs to be removed as well. It doesn’t hold much purpose for the users.

Next Steps

For the next phase, it's important to target the UI aspect of this project and other identified issues the users came across

Here are a few issues that were identified thay needs to be tackled next:

Add "Finger Detection" when logging in. Users will have an easier time entering the app, prevents having to log information frequently.

Change the sizes of the icons, they can be too big and take up too much space. Whilst adding a "Setting" feature

There are too many variations of the same screen. Essentially, the tasks and calendar go to the same page. Just combine the two features.

There are too many edit buttons on each page,this can easily added into the "Setting" page.

"My Tasks" and "Today" seem to be interchangeable. Words and phrases need to be consistent.
